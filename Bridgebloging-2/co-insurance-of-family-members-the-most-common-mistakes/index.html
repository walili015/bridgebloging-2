Co-insurance of family members: the most common mistakes

While family members are usually (co) insured with statutory health and accident insurance, this is often not the case with private insurance. Our LAMIE experts have summarized for you where and when you need to apply for co-insurance, what common mistakes there are and what you should be aware of.  

Mistake No. 1: Children in education are always insured in the statutory health insurance

So only children under the age of 18 are automatically insured with their parents. If the school education takes longer, you can apply for co-insurance under certain conditions. To do this, the child must be under the age of 27, be eligible for family allowance (or provide proof of academic achievement) and he or she must not earn more than the marginal earnings threshold. This also applies to stepchildren, children of choice, grandchildren or foster children.
 

 
In addition, spouses, registered partners and domestic partners who do not have health insurance and other family members who run the household free of charge can also be insured with the statutory health insurance.

Error number 2: Family members are automatically included in the accident insurance

The statutory accident insurance covers people in training, at work and kindergarten children (from the compulsory kindergarten year and for at least 16 hours). Small children, housewives and pensioners do not have this protection.
Benefits for consequential damage from an accident are only paid for accidents at work, in kindergarten, at school, on the way there and at related events. There is no protection for accidents in leisure time , at home, during sports or on vacation.
In addition, family members are not insured with anyone here. Everyone is self-insured as soon as they meet the above requirements. No contributions are collected from the insured; it is financed by the AUVA and a contribution from the family burden compensation fund.

A private family accident insurance (preferably with worldwide coverage) is necessary for adequate accident protection in leisure time and at home.
 

Mistake no. 3: Household and liability insurance: Co-insurance for everyone in the household

In household insurance, including private liability insurance, it is often assumed that children are also insured as long as they live in the same household as their parents, even when they are adults. The fact is, however, that the criteria for co-insurance can be very different and mostly depend on age (usually up to 25 or 27 years of age), marital status, education or whether family allowances are received. If the child starts working life, the co-insurance usually expires. Children who are gainfully employed or who do not meet the above criteria must be included in the insurance contract if they still live at home.

Many insurers offer to insure the children’s household effects at the place of study. However, this protection is usually limited and applies to student dormitories, boarding schools or possibly to shared apartments. In addition, some of the exceptions are that the insurance cover only applies to liability, but not to the contents of the apartment. If this is the case, a separate household insurance should be taken out for the student apartment , where all roommates including their household are insured.

In general, only family members and partners are insured free of charge in household insurance. So if you live with a friend, he usually has to be included in the contract.

LAMIE tip : In the LAMIE direct household  insurance including liability insurance, the  contents of all residents registered in the household are automatically insured.
 

Misconception No. 4: In the event of death, family members are entitled to private life and pension insurance

At the latest when building a house and starting a family, you should think about an end and / or life insurance. Particularly important here is the lump-sum settlement, with which, for example, mortgages can be repaid in the event of death and the surviving dependents can thus have a debt-free apartment to live on.
In the case of an existing or a new life insurance or private pension insurance, a specific person can be included in the contract as a beneficiary. The sum is paid directly to this beneficiary. Otherwise, the life insurance of a deceased falls into the estate and is divided among the family members in accordance with Austrian inheritance law .

Misconception No. 5: Co-insurance of family members with credit card travel insurance

With many credit card insurances, family members are also insured when traveling. At least that is always highlighted. However, if you look at the details of the various conditions, you will notice that certain services – such as important travel health insurance – are often limited to the cardholder. You can find more about this in our large credit card travel insurance comparison .

With other private insurances such as annual travel insurance, legal expenses insurance or private health insurance, you must also insure family members separately or take out family insurance.
