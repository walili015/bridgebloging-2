Bicycle theft: what does household insurance pay?

A great feeling: Summer is here and you can finally get your beloved bike out of hibernation. However, especially in the warm season, it is not exactly safe for your bike outside. Bicycles are constantly changing hands without permission – 20,805 bicycles were stolen in Austria in 2019, according to VCÖ. But how can you adequately insure your bike and what does household insurance cover? LAMIE enlightened!

Bicycle thieves are not only out and about in public places, but also in residential complexes. The household insurance usually provides good basic protection against bicycle theft in their own home. In principle, however, insurance cover only exists if your bike is either locked or locked. This means that it “must” be stolen in the following way, so to speak:

  • if you break into your apartment or a locked cellar, or
  • in the event of theft from a general part of the building (bicycle cellar, attic, corridor, garden), if it was previously secured with a lock.

It is clear to many that the bike has to be locked in some way and it goes without saying anyway. What is unfortunately not so clear as day: The insurance coverage of your bicycle from the household insurance ends at the property line!

Bicycle theft – WHEN and WHERE does household insurance get out?

There is no insurance cover:

  • in the event of bicycle theft outside the insured property (applies to the street in front of the house)
  • in the event of theft of unlocked bicycles from communal areas (such as attics, cellars, storage rooms, staircases)
  • mostly in the case of partial theft, i.e. if only parts of the bike are stolen (saddle, handlebars, accessories, and the like)

Danger! The compensation from household insurance in the event of bicycle theft is relatively low at an average of € 1,500 per claim. In the case of expensive bicycles or e-bikes, separate bicycle insurance could be worthwhile. More on this below.

 Take precautions with appropriate documentation! Invoice receipts and photos facilitate the processing in the event of a claim.

A separate bicycle insurance for high-quality bicycles and e-bikes 

If, however, you travel a lot by bike and most of the time it is cordoned off in other places, you should consider getting your own bike theft insurance. The range of services is more extensive than household insurance and usually also includes partial theft and vandalism. For e-bikes there is even comprehensive fully and partially comprehensive insurance, which also includes natural hazards, fire and accidents.
The sports equipment should really be worth such an insurance. Because the premiums are usually relatively expensive and deductibles or annual discounts are to be expected. Usually only new bicycles can be insured. Some insurers require a certain minimum value of the bicycle lock, as well as the registration of the bicycle.

Protective measures: How to protect your bike from thieves

  • Under no circumstances should you save on the bike lock!
  • Use fixed barriers / railings (under no circumstances attach the bike lock only between the frame and the tire)
  • Lock the bike directly on the frame! (The wheel can be easily removed)
  • Use safety screws (saddle and front wheel are often only fitted with a quick release)
  • Park your bike in busy places
  • Register your bike http://www.fase24.com/
