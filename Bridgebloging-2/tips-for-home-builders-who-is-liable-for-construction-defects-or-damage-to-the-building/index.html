Tips for home builders: Who is liable for construction defects or damage to the building?

If you want to build a house or have it built, you should always be prepared for construction defects, postponements and the associated , protracted disputes with construction companies . Because these are not uncommon in construction practice. A lack of expert knowledge and a lack of consultation between construction companies can quickly turn the dream of a house into a nightmare. In addition, construction defects are not apparent to a layperson and damage can only become apparent years later. We clarify who is liable and what you can do in the event of construction defects or damage. 

In the event that you are not so familiar with the terms used in construction, we have two important definitions for you to begin with . 

Client 

The builder is the person who is responsible for the preparation or execution of development on a property or who commissions this. If you have bought a plot of land and would like to have a house built on it, you are the owner. 

Site manager and site manager 

The builder himself (if he has the necessary training) or a person representing him (e.g. from a building or general contractor ) can be appointed as site manager. He coordinated the site , ensures that all construction companies such as plumbers, roofers , Ele k triker etc. e fficient work together , controlled compliance with the requirements, costs, and is the contact person for ” home builders ” .  

The site manager is a subordinate employee of the site manager , can represent him to a certain extent and is responsible for the implementation of partial tasks on the construction site. Both the site manager and site manager must be notified to the client before the work begins 1 

What can construction companies be held liable for ? 

A construction company is liable to the client for quality, deadlines and defects. If a general contractor is commissioned, it also bears the economic and organizational risk.

But responsibility is not only towards one’s own work, but in some ways also to d s preparations other personnel working at the same site sub-firms . Namely, the own work on a defective preliminary work on construction , threatening the building contractor at a loss , the loss of one’s right to compensation for work , even if the job basically was carried out properly . So there is a certain duty to warn the builder and not just quietly respond to a r unclean basis.
 

 

What to do in the event of construction defects ? 

The biggest problem with building defects that they are often hidden and L a ien not be recognized under normal circumstances, at least not before it is too late or already damage occurred. Then what’s the best way to get your money back? First of all, there is an important distinction between warranty and compensation with regard to when the defect is discovered. 

Guarantee 

The property developer is entitled to a warranty for 3 years from handover, but for the thing itself, i.e. for the defect, not for the consequential damage. If construction defects are recognizable after a certain period of time (within 3 years) , the buyer must prove that these were already present at the time of handover. It is therefore always advisable to precisely document any construction progress using photos.
However, it is legally assumed that a defect that occurs within six months of handover already existed at the time of handover. Depending on the type and severity of the defect, the buyer or dthe client assert the rectification of defects, replacement, price reduction or repayment of the purchase price. The best case here would of course be to identify the defect before paying. As long as there is a defect, the house does not have to be taken over and the payment to the respective construction company does not have to be made.
So it gives an idea of ​​how complicated things can get. At the slightest suspicion of improper construction, or a tree Angels you should immediately contact an independent expert. 
 

 

Compensation 

Entitled to compensation has one only if already a damage has occurred and it is proved by means verifier that the underlying tree Angel the cause for this was is. The period from knowledge of the damage and the damaging party is also 3 years. For this it is important that everything is well documented and that all indications point to the guilt of the respective construction company. As soon as wear and tear are involved, or there is an extraordinary chain of circumstances, this could lead to a reduction in liability or even exclusion of liability from construction companies .There is one major benefit to you, however. As previously mentioned, claims for damages only become statute-barred after three years FROM KNOWLEDGE of the damage, but at most after 30 years from acceptance of the structure. In other words, if the damage only occurs after 10 years, i.e. long after the existing warranty period, then you have 3 years from this point in time to assert claims through compensation law. 

How do I protect myself against unpredictable events? 

Construction insurance 

Not all damage can be traced back to human error, and the whims of nature can quickly cause major problems. One way for building owners to protect themselves extensively is construction insurance.

Such a “construction comprehensive insurance” covers on the one hand consequential damage caused by natural events such as Rain, storm, hail, falling rocks, earthquakes and much more. and on the other hand damage that can also be attributed to the building contractor. This includes, among other things, consequential damage caused by execution errors, clumsiness, failure of supporting structures or falling objects.  

Examples: 

  • Natural hazards: The excavation pit is full due to heavy rain; the properly secured construction crane falls over in a raging storm and damages the roof. 
  • Unsuitable execution by the construction company: Due to the too thin foundation, the house tilts / rises or falls. 
Insurance contract liability 

It is also possible to secure the property for a period of 36 months after completion of the construction. The so-called “Extended Maintenance” covers damage that originated during the construction period (such as leaky pipelines). 

It often happens, however, that damage appears much later, although it occurred much earlier. If the ( property ) damage due to incorrect construction was already set in the construction stage, this is usually covered even after the insurance cover has expired . In any case, you can report the damage to your insurance company within 36 months – this is also the duration of the warranty for buildings. 

Building insurance has a significant advantage for the builder, because he does not have to deal with the trade, but can coordinate directly with the insurer. 

Shell insurance 

Another way to cover yourself would be to take out shell insurance. A shell insurance protects you against damage caused by force majeure such as. Fire and storm have arisen.   

Advantage: When you take out home insurance, many insurers give you shell insurance free of charge. This means that the first premium is usually only due when the house is ready for occupancy. 

Damage to third parties  

As the client, you have the duty to secure the construction site. Among other things, you must ensure that no one is injured or property is damaged. If damage occurs to the neighbors or other third parties as a result of a construction site, the builder is fully liable to the injured party, even without guilt. Private individuals are liable as builders with their private assets. 

A sensible protection for this would be a builder’s liability insurance . This protects you from financial burdens caused by claims for damages that are made against you as the client. A possible damage scenario would be, for example. the damage to the neighboring building due to excavation.  

Even after the completion of the building and now a proud homeowner, incidents can occur for which you are liable to third parties for damages. 

Examples:  

  • Someone is slipping on your property because you did not clear the snow and therefore violated the traffic safety obligation. 
  • A tree in the garden falls on a car parked in front of the house. 

In order to be protected in such situations, it is advisable to take out home and property liability insurance . 

Info: When you take out homeowner insurance, you can usually include home and property liability as a component in the contract. Find out more about this from your insurer. 

Basically, it can be said that defects or damage that occur, which are becoming more and more common despite improved building materials and technologies , are not easy to drink and involve a lot of effort, time and often also legal disputes. Therefore, the top rule is to always agree on reasonable payment methods and never pay in advance. It also avoids losing a lot of money in the event of a construction company going bankrupt.
