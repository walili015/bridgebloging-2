6 Helpful tips for a safe autumn around the home and car

As soon as Indian summer has passed, autumn usually immediately lets us feel its presence. The changed weather conditions alone bring with them a wide variety of dangers, which you have to adjust to every year. We have put together a list of the most important to-dos and insurance tips for you so that you can get through autumn safely.

Living

1. Twilight time is break-in time

Due to the early twilight in the months of October to March, burglars strike particularly often between 4 p.m. and 9 p.m. According to the police, the apartments and houses affected are usually well connected to main traffic routes, which enables the perpetrators to swear quickly. The focus is on residential areas with hard-to-see properties and apartments at ground level.

Simple tips for protection against break-ins, especially at dusk, include:

  • Use timers and illuminate basement exits.
  • Attach motion detectors.
  • Install the most complete outdoor lighting possible .
  • Check for typical break-in vulnerabilities .

 What you need to look out for so that household insurance pays in the event of a break-in can be found in our blog post on the subject of burglary .

2. Clear leaves

According to § 93 StVO, homeowners are obliged to clear public sidewalks, sidewalks and stairs adjacent to their own property. Although these provisions are mostly only associated with the classic “snow removal and gritting obligations” in winter, the removal of leaves also falls under this obligation, for example when wet leaves endanger the safety of pedestrians. Renters take note – homeowners can delegate the responsibility of clearing the sidewalk to their tenants. Then these stick if, for example, a pedestrian slips in front of the house and injures himself.
Attention: It is forbidden to sweep the leaves from the sidewalk onto the street if this endangers road safety (§ 92 StVO).


 
 Also, don’t forget to clear private walkways on your property. As a signpost, you are responsible for ensuring that pavements can be used safely by authorized persons.

Drive

3. Foliage and aquaplaning

Early fog, rain showers and wind – even a driver has to get used to the gruesome autumn weather. Leaves lying around or heavy rainfalls in autumn can cause a lot of sliding on some roads. Extreme caution is required on wet roads and in poor visibility. Keep your distance and expect a longer braking distance!

 Do you think that none of this is a problem with your comprehensive insurance? Do not lull yourself into a false sense of security. In the event of accident damage to your own car that you yourself caused negligently, for example because you did not reduce your speed sufficiently despite heavy rain, the comprehensive insurance is not obliged to pay for the damage. However, some insurance companies offer protection in the event of gross negligence for an additional charge. Check your insurance contract!

4. Game crossing

In autumn, drivers must expect more deer passages at dawn and dusk – especially in rural areas.

Warning: If you brake because of an animal, you risk contributing to a rear-end collision. Contributory negligence means that you then bear part of the damage yourself or have to compensate the driver for part of the damage. Whether the braking maneuver is justified and not contributing to your negligence depends on the size of the animal. The larger the animal, the more likely it is that the danger to self and property justifies an emergency stop; for example with deer, stag and large dogs; but not with hedgehogs, rabbits and cats.

 If you can no longer avoid “Bambi”, the comprehensive insurance pays for the damage to your own car. The game damage must be reported to the police immediately.

5. Rockfall

As soon as the road conditions get worse in autumn, the gravel also increases on the road. Then it can happen that a pebble is caught by the tire of a vehicle and thrown against the windshield of the vehicle behind it.

 The comprehensive insurance covers damage to windshields due to falling rocks. Without comprehensive insurance, you can only claim damages from the motor vehicle liability insurance of the vehicle in front if he is driving on the shoulder of the road or driving at excessive speed in poor road conditions.

6. Tire change

In Austria, winter tires are compulsory from November 1st to April 15th in winter driving conditions (snow, mud or ice). For more safety in poor road conditions and colder temperatures (less than 7 ° C), it is advisable to convert to winter tires beforehand. Because the higher profile depth of the winter tires (at least 4 mm) and the softer rubber compound ensure better traction.

Heavy rains, hail and storms are not uncommon even in autumn. Our blog post on the topic of storms tells you what you need to think about so that the insurance pays in the event of damage.
