Sports equipment: what insurances pay?

People invest a lot of time and money in many a hobby or passion. Sports equipment such as bicycles, skis, golf equipment or fishing equipment are of high material value and are therefore popular stolen goods. But you also want to have your beloved sports equipment covered against other damage, which is why sports equipment insurance is increasingly being offered when you buy it. But what exactly is insured and when do other insurances also protect? We’ll briefly clarify this for you.

What does household insurance cover?

In the LAMIE household  insurance, sports equipment is insured against damage (fire, storm etc.) and burglary within the insured premises. For a burglary, however, the device must be inside the locked apartment or a locked cellar. If the sports equipment is parked in common areas (for example in the bike cellar, staircase or garden), it must be cordoned off accordingly. This means that the burglar or thief must demonstrably break something in order for the insurance to pay.
Caution:For certain devices, insurance cover can also be restricted (sublimits). Motorized devices such as quards, golf carts and jetsky are usually excluded. So check your insurance conditions.
 

 

Damage to third parties

If someone else is harmed by an accident or carelessness while exercising with your own device, private liability insurance generally pays. This is usually included in the household insurance. However, this does not apply if, for example, you handle a friend’s sports equipment and damage it in the process. This is a so-called activity damage, which is usually excluded.

When is sports equipment insurance worthwhile?

Sports equipment insurance covers property damage and is a kind of comprehensive insurance, so to speak. Typical sports equipment insurances on the market are insurance for fishing equipment, bicycles, ski and snowboards, golf equipment, hunting and sporting weapons, and diving equipment. In particular, pay attention to whether theft or burglary is insured and what the requirements for benefits in the event of a claim are. In the case of device insurance policies that cover simple theft, it is often the case that the device will be rejected due to gross negligence if the device is not closely guarded at all times.
 

 
To ensure that the device is insured against damage while on the move or when traveling, it should include transport insurance or the insurance cover should generally also be valid abroad.

Sports equipment insurance generally makes sense when investing in expensive sports equipment. Unfortunately, the price-performance ratio is often poor and repairs are cheaper. Here you shouldn’t conclude directly in the shop, but rather do the calculations again at home or compare different offers.

On vacation: sports equipment protected by travel insurance?

Whether sports equipment is covered in luggage insurance (often also included in credit card travel insurance) always depends on how “luggage” is defined there. According to most conditions, luggage includes things that are taken with you on trips for personal and private use for personal travel needs. The bike or the fishing rod are included here, depending on the type of trip (examples are a bike tour through Vietnam or a fishing trip to Norway).
 
In principle, luggage is insured against theft in common travel insurances, but only in connection with a break-in (e.g. a locked car was broken into). Damage caused by fire and natural hazards are also mostly damage (Damage caused by natural forces) or insured by a traffic accident.
