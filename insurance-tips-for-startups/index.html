Insurance tips for startups

Land of mountains, land on the river, land of bureaucracy. Starting a business in Austria is an excursion into the jungle of uncertainty. Often this overlooks the need to adequately insure yourself or the company. This can be a fatal mistake if, for example, unexpected events turn the dream of a startup and self-employed money into a nightmare. To avoid this risk, we have summarized five tips for you on the subject of insurance for startups.

1. Think about insurance: an often forgotten step in self-employment

It may surprise one or the other, but many entrepreneurs do not even think about insurance for the company for the time being. The reasons for this are, on the one hand, ignorance, because very few are aware of what insurance there is for entrepreneurs and which one is needed. On the other hand, many do not even think about it during their career as founders because they are completely absorbed by other problems or tasks. Especially in the start-up phase, an entrepreneur looks at financing and customer growth. Is understandable. But you should also consider which risks could hit you particularly hard at the beginning.

2. Analyze industry-specific risks – which insurance makes sense?

In order to weigh up from the outset which type of insurance would be suitable, it is essential to carry out a short risk analysis. It is a good idea to categorize your industry-specific risks into low, medium, and high risks. Each industry therefore also needs different insurance. Disaster risks that would mean the end of the company should be hedged first and foremost. In the case of transport companies , the transport should therefore be insured with good transport liability and public liability insurance, in the event that, for example, large quantities of goods are employed. A professional liability insurance would be analogous to thismakes sense for an IT company because programming errors, for example, can quickly cause major damage. If high investments have been made in plant and equipment, these should be covered with tailor-made property insurance including business interruption.

3. Industry-independent risk – cyber crime

While risks are largely industry-specific, there are some hazards that apply to most industries. The most rapidly growing threat can be found in the IT sector. Cunning cyber attacks can quickly paralyze entire websites or result in complete data loss. For a young startup, this quickly means financial ruin if the trust that has been so painstakingly built up is lost with the first important customers. In addition to investing in cyber security in good time, proper password protection and educating employees against social engineering attacks, you should also take out cyber insurance.
Tip : The GDV offers a  cyber security check with which you can assess how good the IT security of your company is.
 

 

4. General insurance for startups

The following is a brief overview of the most important general insurances in the start-up area:

Public liability insurance

This is the corporate counterpart to personal liability insurance. As a result, the company and the associated employees are insured when carrying out their operational activities if third-party property or persons are negligently damaged. This means that claims for damages are checked first and if there is a justified claim for damages, the damage will be settled by this insurance to the extent of the coverage. Business liability insurance is particularly useful for companies with several employees. Typically, such incidents often happen in skilled trades, where errors occur in the work process and these subsequently affect the customer.

Professional Liability Insurance

This is an industry-specific insurance that is relevant for many self-employed and also freelancers. This is especially true for industries with serious consequential errors. In addition, there are also professionals who complete by law to hold professional indemnity insurance must . Professional liability insurance usually differs from public liability insurance in terms of the insurance coverage. As a rule, it covers pure financial loss, with public liability aimed at personal injury and property damage. The professional groups mentioned above include, for example, tax consultants, lawyers and architects. This type of insurance is also highly recommended for startups with risky errors, such as in the IT industry.

D&O insurance

Key persons should also be protected accordingly, because they usually run a very high risk. Managing directors and managers are liable to the company for culpably committed breaches of duty with their private assets. If one acts negligently as a managing director and thereby falls into the liability of the managing director, it can in some cases lead to bankruptcy. Directors and officers insurance protects the private assets of the manager and the assets of the executives.
 

 

Company legal protection insurance

Professional liability insurance already covers passive legal protection, while corporate legal protection takes on active legal protection. In the event of a lawsuit, the company legal protection insurance will cover the legal costs, which include court and lawyer fees.

In order to find an insurance tailored to your own needs, an advisor can be useful to explain possible options. The Integral Insurance Broker GmbH will be happy to help and advice.

5. Company pension schemes and private pension insurance

As a founder of a startup, you actually live the progress of the future. The only thing that is often not considered is getting older, although founders also have an enormous amount of personal responsibility here.

In Austria there are statutory, private and company pension schemes. The latter is only available for employees, which is why the self-employed usually have to make provisions with a high private share. Options for this are long-term investments in real estate or stocks, traditionally saving money for old age or investing in good private pension insurance. As part of the pension commitment, you can convert business assets into private assets for indispensable workers or for the company owner himself, by investing in pension provision insurance.

But pension funds or company group insurance are also a good measure in terms of personnel policy for employees of start-ups. The additional financial and solid provision for the future is an incentive for many young people. Which is important considering the fact that small, young companies often have a competitive disadvantage when it comes to pay. When investing in a company pension scheme, the premium payments are not only exempt from social security contributions and other ancillary wage costs, but are also deductible as business expenses.
